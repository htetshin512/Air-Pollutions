What’s affecting Yangon’s air?

Identifying major pollution drivers to empower a cleaner urban environment and healthier community lifestyle.

Vehicle Emissions

The growing number of cars and buses release high levels of carbon monoxide and PM2.5. Traffic congestion in Yangon compounds this, forcing engines to burn fuel inefficiently at idle.

Industrial Zones

Manufacturing hubs release chemical gases and particulate matter via fuel combustion. These industrial activities are primary contributors to urban smog and environmental degradation.

Seasonal Burning

Burning agricultural waste and household leaves releases toxic smoke. During the dry season, pollutants remain trapped in the lower atmosphere, spikes pollution levels sharply.

Deep Breakdown

Explore the structural reasons behind Yangon's changing atmospheric conditions.

Urbanization leads to increased construction dust and higher energy demands, which significantly lower clean air benchmarks in residential districts.

Diesel generators, used during grid outages, emit concentrated sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides directly into the street-level breathing zones.

Insufficient waste collection leads to community burning, which is the primary source of hazardous smoke in many local townships.

Did you know?

Pollution in Yangon peaks during the **dry season** (Dec–May).

Stagnant air and lack of rain allow fine particles to accumulate, creating a "dome" effect of pollutants over the city center.
