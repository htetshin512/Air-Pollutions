Atmospheric Science

Understanding Air Quality and Pollution

Nitrogen (78%)

Nitrogen makes up around 78% of Earth’s atmosphere and helps maintain stable environmental conditions. It is naturally present in clean air and supports ecological balance.

Oxygen (21%)

Oxygen makes up about 21% of the atmosphere and is essential for breathing and human survival. Healthy air quality helps maintain safe oxygen levels in urban environments.

Particulate Matter (PM2.5/PM10)

PM2.5 and PM10 are tiny air pollutants produced by traffic, burning waste, factories, and dust. These particles can enter the lungs and bloodstream, increasing health risks.

Understanding Air Pollution

Learn how air pollution forms, how it is measured, and how it affects public health and the urban environment.

Understanding Air Quality Index (AQI)

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is an international system used to show how clean or polluted the air is at a certain time. As AQI levels rise, the health risks also increase, especially for children, older adults, and people with asthma or breathing problems. High AQI levels can cause coughing, eye irritation, and difficulty breathing.

Sources of Urban Air Pollution

In Yangon, air pollution mainly comes from vehicle exhaust, factory emissions, construction dust, diesel generators, and open waste burning. These activities release harmful pollutants such as PM2.5 and toxic gases into the atmosphere, lowering air quality and creating haze across urban areas.

How we breathe the Air

Tiny pollutants such as PM2.5 are small enough to enter deep into the lungs and even the bloodstream when people breathe polluted air. Long-term exposure can damage the respiratory system and increase the risk of asthma, lung infections, heart disease, and other serious health conditions.
