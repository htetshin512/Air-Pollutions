Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about Yangon air quality, AQI colors, and how EcoSphere works.

PM2.5 refers to extremely small air pollution particles that are less than 2.5 micrometers wide. Because they are so tiny, they can enter deep into the lungs and even the bloodstream, affecting human health.
Major causes include vehicle emissions, industrial smoke, construction dust, diesel generators, and open waste burning. Pollution levels can become worse during dry seasons with low rainfall and weak wind circulation.
Air pollution may cause coughing, throat irritation, breathing difficulties, asthma attacks, and eye discomfort. Long-term exposure can increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and lung damage.
AQI stands for Air Quality Index. It measures how clean or polluted the air is and whether it may affect people’s health. Higher AQI values mean higher pollution levels and greater health risks.
People can wear N95 or KN95 masks, reduce outdoor activities, close windows during severe pollution periods, and regularly check AQI updates.
Trees and plants help absorb carbon dioxide, trap dust particles, and improve overall air quality in urban areas. Green spaces also help cool cities naturally.
Smart city solutions include real-time AQI monitoring, sustainable transportation, urban greening, cleaner energy use, and digital environmental awareness systems.
